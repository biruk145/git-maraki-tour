Harenna Forest

The Harenna Forest is a montane tropical evergreen forest in Ethiopia's Bale Mountains. The forest covers the southern slope of the mountains, extending from 1450 to 3200 meters elevation. The Bale Mountains are in Ethiopia's Oromia Region, and form the southwestern portion of the Ethiopian Highlands.

Lalibela

Lalibela ( ላሊበላ, romanized: Lalibäla) is a town in the Amhara Region of Ethiopia. Located in the Lasta district and North Wollo Zone, it is a tourist site for its famous rock-cut monolithic churches designed in contrast to the earlier monolithic churches in Ethiopia.

Axum

Axum (or Aksum) is an ancient city in Ethiopia's Tigray region and a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for the Axumite Empire and its massive granite stelae, royal tombs, and a sacred site for Ethiopian Christianity. The city was the capital of a powerful ancient kingdom that dominated the Red Sea trade and was once one of the four great powers of the ancient world.

Nile River

The Nile River's primary Ethiopian headwaters are the Abāy River, or Blue Nile, which originates at Lake Tana and provides roughly 85% of the main Nile's water, particularly during the rainy season. The Blue Nile flows through Ethiopia's highlands, forming a spectacular canyon, before joining the White Nile in Khartoum, Sudan, and eventually reaching the Mediterranean Sea

Gonder Fasildes

Fasiledes Gondar refers to Fasil Ghebbi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Gondar, Ethiopia, that is a fortified complex of palaces, churches, and public buildings built by Emperor Fasilides in the 17th century. Fasilides (also spelled Fasiladas) established Gondar as a permanent capital, and his royal enclosure, known as the Royal Enclosure, became the center of Ethiopia's imperial court for over 200 years.

Debre LIbanose

Debre Libanos (ደብረ ሊባኖስ) is an Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo monastery, lying northwest of Addis Ababa in the North Shewa Zone of the Oromia Region. It was founded in 1284 by Saint Tekle Haymanot as Debre Atsbo and was renamed as Debre Libanos in the 15th century. He prayed in a cave above the current monastery for 29 years. The monastery's chief abbot, called the Ichege, was the second most powerful official in the Ethiopian Church after the Abuna.
